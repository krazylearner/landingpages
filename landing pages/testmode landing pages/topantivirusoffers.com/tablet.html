Protecting your Tablet from Viruses

While any tablet can get infected by a malicious computer virus, some computer analysts say that Android devices might be particularly vulnerable because the software is released as open source. That means anybody can download the source code and examine it for flaws. However, it isn't safe to assume that Apple devices are invulnerable either. Both devices make an attractive target for criminals who hope to steal information or simply create mischief.

Do You Need Virus Protection on a Tablet?

Very often, hackers exploit vulnerabilities in downloadable software. While recognized brands might provide safer choices, even popular companies have been victims of code exploits that have infected thousands of computers.

The practice of downloading free apps, especially from unknown developers, is much riskier. There is a chance that freeware won't get monitored as well. In the worst cases, the app maker might even intentionally use their product as a way to transfer viruses to user's tablets. By doing this, the criminals might just be trying to prove their skills, but they also might be after sensitive personal information.

Best Practices for Tablet Virus Protection

The best defenses against tablet varieties include using both technology and your own common sense.

  1. Mostly Download Tablet Apps From Official Sources
    2. The best practice is to only download apps from the official app store unless you know for sure that another source of apps is safe. For example, Android users should use Google Play. People with Amazon devices should confine themselves to the Amazon Appstore.

    Most of the time, infected apps come from unrecognized, third-party download sites. Of course, reputable businesses do sell apps from third-party sites, so if you choose to use one, be sure you carefully research both the site and the app. No matter where you plan to download your app from, it is always a good idea to research it by reading some online tablet app reviews.

  2. Install Tablet Virus Protection
    3. Similar to PC antivirus software, tablet antivirus protection can check your downloads and currently running applications for viruses. Apps are different, but some may block infected downloads, alert users to problem, and even attempt to wipe out the virus. A number of the biggest names in security provide tablet virus protection, and you should take the time to read online virus protection reviews to see which one seems like it would work the best for you. Of course, you should also only download your security app from a trusted source.

Do You Really Need to Install Tablet Virus Protection?

If you take reasonable care to keep your device locked and only download trusted apps, you are probably better protected than a great percentage of other tablet computer users. However, if you loan your device to your kids, friends, or even elderly parents, you could be taking a big risk if they aren't as aware of the dangers. Good tablet security can give you peace of mind and protect your investment and personal information.
