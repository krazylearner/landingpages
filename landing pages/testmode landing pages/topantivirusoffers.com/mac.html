Do Macs Need Virus Protection?

There is often confusion and debate around how, and if, Mac users should protect their machines from viruses and other malicious attempts to hijack and steal personal data. Find out the truth in our article.

Do Macs need virus protection?

The simple answer is yes, Macs are susceptible to virus attacks, but PC related viruses are what we generally hear about in the media. PCs have maintained the market share since the introduction of Windows, and it has been more lucrative for hackers to target PCs.

Creators of malicious software which can infect your machine with viruses, are more likely to create a program that runs on the Windows platform because Mac works under a different operating system. The Unix system Mac uses would require the creator to code two unique pieces of software and then attempt to target each user with the appropriate version. This is much more difficult for the makers of viruses and other malicious programs, which offers Mac users more inherent security.

However, just because it requires more work to create a virus for Mac users doesn't mean they are safe from harm. Macs are growing in popularity at a rapid rate and specially created viruses are likely to become more and more common as the market share of Mac machines increases.

It's also important to remind Mac users that although their systems have many valuable built-in security features, they must still be vigilant about their safety. For example, in February 2014 a major security flaw, known as Gate Keeper, was discovered in Mac Machines. This security hole made personal data on a users machine accessible to anyone when the user was connected to a Wifi hotspot.

In the end, regardless of which type of computer you use. It's important to remain vigilant. Developing best practices for safely surfing the web will considerably cut down your risk for downloading a malicious program and becoming infected with a virus. To ensure you are adopting safe practices online, make sure you are careful about who and when you provide any personal information - even legitimate looking sources may be fakes designed to steal your data.
