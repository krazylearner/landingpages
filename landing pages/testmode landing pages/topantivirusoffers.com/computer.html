What is a computer virus?


A computer virus is a deliberate bug in your system which infects your computer and performs malicious acts. Viruses are created to do many things, but most commonly they result in the following:

  • Loss of data
  • Loss of privacy
  • Poor computer performance
  • Potential loss of personally identifiable information.

Viruses are subtle. They try to trick you into downloading them by masquerading as files both harmless and helpful. As technology has advanced, you don’t even have to click a “download” button to have your computer infiltrated. Common entrances include email attachments and web links; however, they can also piggy-back on document macros, installation procedures, and other programs.

A computer virus is a subset in the umbrella term of “malware,” or malicious software. Computer viruses are technically self-replicating programs. Other malicious programs include:

  • Trojans: comes disguised as useful programs.
  • Worms: can replicate across networks and infect the computers of friends, family or more.
  • Ransomware: encrypts and takes over a computer and demands payment for its release.
  • Keyloggers: records your every keystroke, and can steal passwords, banking information and more.
  • Adware or software packages which display advertisements, possibly outside of a browser.

What Are Symptoms of a Computer Virus?

So, of more immediate concern than the definitions, what are computer virus symptoms? Computer viruses are deliberately crafted for the purpose of disrupting computer operation, stealing information, or hijacking your computer.

Here are some basic symptoms that might indicate your computer has a virus:

  • Poor performance and peed decreases
  • Unexplained freezing or crashing
  • Programs that won’t launch or install.
  • Failure to boot or turn on.
  • Changes in computer settings
  • Disappearance of files or folders
  • Pop-ups and ads appearing on your desktop
  • Hard drive is working when you’re not on the computer.
  • People get emails from you that you didn’t send

How to Prevent Computer Viruses

It’s important to prevent computer viruses through installation of antivirus software as well as adopting best practices while surfing the internet. Below are some best practices for ensuring you surf the web safely:

  • Don’t click on suspicious links.
  • Be wary of opening emails from senders you are unfamiliar with.
  • Do not download e-mail attachments from people you do not personally know.
  • Be careful when downloading programs from websites; be sure it is a trusted website you are downloading from.
  • If you receive an email from a company you do business with, go to their website directly rather than clicking on the links provided.
