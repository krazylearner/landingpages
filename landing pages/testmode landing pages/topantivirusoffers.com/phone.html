Viruses on Mobile Phones: What are the Signs?

Reading these statistics, you'll want to know if your phone is safe. Here are the top signs to watch for:

  • Phone acting slow
  • Lots of internet usage
  • Sudden surge in phone data usage, when your browsing habits haven't changed
  • Apps present that you did not install

Can You Get a Virus on a Cell Phone?

The short answer here is yes. Are you likely to get a virus on a cell phone? That's a different question.

If you have an Android device, you're much likelier to have a problem than if you use another device. According to existing data, iPhone, BlackBerry, and Windows Phone users have a less than 1 percent risk of getting a virus on their phone.

If you download apps from the authorized retailer - like the App Store or Google Play - then your risk of downloading an app that contains a virus is very low.

Most malware gets on devices when users download files from third-party app stores in the hopes of getting a popular game like Candy Crush for free. Instead of saving a couple dollars, they end up downloading a malicious app and getting a virus on their phone. Malware can also get on your mobile device through email, SMS, or suspicious website visits, but this is less common.

As a best practice, avoid any third-party retailers and only buy authorized apps that have been scanned and deemed safe by major app marketplaces.

If you don't use an Android, and you don't download many apps - or any apps from unauthorized retailers - you might not need antivirus software for your phone. If you do want to protect yourself, here's how.

How to Protect Your Phone From Viruses

There are antivirus apps you can place on your phone. These apps will scan your phone and alert you to anything suspicious. Some come with other helpful features, like automated data backup or the ability to find your phone when it becomes lost.

Protecting your phone from viruses is as easy as installing the app, keeping your phone's software updated so it has the latest protections, and following recommended practices to minimize your exposure to malware in the first place.

If a virus is detected, put your phone in airplane mode to stop transmissions. Then delete the app. If you are unable to manually delete it, try doing so using your phone's safe mode. If this does not work, you may need to wipe the device to get rid of the virus.

While it's your decision whether you need antivirus for your phone, knowing the symptoms and statistics can help you be an informed app shopper and just may you stay safe.
